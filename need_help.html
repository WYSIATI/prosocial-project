911


    Dial 911

    When to call:

     -When the condition is life or limb threatening
    -If their condition could worsen and become life-threatening on the way to the hospital
    -If moving the individual could cause further injury
    -If the individual needs the skills or equipment of the paramedics or EMT
    -If distance or traffic conditions would cause a delay in getting the individual to the hospital

    If it’s potentially life threatening, when in doubt, call 911

    Counseling Services


      902-494-2081

       Location: LeMarchant Mixed Use Facility, 1246 Lemarchant St. 2nd Floor, Room 2102

      Hours of operation:
       Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday: 9 a.m.– 4:45 p.m.
      Wednesday: 9 a.m.– 8:15 p.m.
      Saturday and Sunday: closed

      http://www.dal.ca/campus_life/student_services/health-and-wellness/counselling/personal-counselling.html

      Offers Dalhousie student’s free access to qualified counselors and psychologists. They have a limited number of emergency appointments and sometimes it can take up to several weeks before you can be seen. They do not do drop-ins – you must make an appointment. If you are unable to wait this long, please go to health services who are also able to help you address these issues.

      Avg. wait time: 6-8 weeks for “regular priority”; one week for emergency
